My Story

Hi, I'm Jeyadave Nuntha Kumar, a passionate Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) engineer and researcher with a strong interest in complex flows, turbulence modeling, and higher order numerical methods.

My love for CFD bloomed during my undergraduate research at the Glenn L. Martin Wind Tunnel located at the University of Maryland. Jumping into such a complex and computationally demanding project was one of my hardest academic challenges, but one that allowed me to channel my appreciation for aerodynamics and fluid mechanics into tough, unsolved problems. Since then, I have been captivated by all aspects of CFD, including those often visited by applied mathematicians and computer scientists.

I've been privileged to work on impactful aerospace research and foster inclusivity in STEM through mentorship and leadership. My multicultural experiences spanning the USA and Singapore have instilled a global perspective and appreciation for diverse ideas.

Outside of work, I enjoy playing classical and anime music on the piano, discovering new food, and watching Star Trek.
