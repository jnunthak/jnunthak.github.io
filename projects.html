Project Title 1

Introduction

This project explores the design and construction of a balsa wood R/C glider to navigate an obstacle course. The focus was on optimizing lift-to-drag ratio and structural integrity.

Method

The glider was designed using CAD software, and aerodynamic performance was simulated with XFOIL. Prototyping included multiple iterations for balance and control surface tuning.

Results

The final model successfully completed the obstacle course, demonstrating superior handling and glide efficiency compared to initial prototypes.

Project Title 2

Introduction

This project involved developing a simulation tool for modeling flow patterns around a wing with Gurney flaps in a wind tunnel.

Method

Using STAR-CCM+, the simulations evaluated the aerodynamic effects of varying Gurney flap heights. Results were validated using experimental wind tunnel data.

Results

The study demonstrated that optimal flap heights could increase lift by up to 25%, paving the way for improved aerodynamic designs in aviation.

Project Title 3

Introduction

A custom-built flight simulator project aimed at enhancing pilot training capabilities and exploring realistic control dynamics.

Method

Leveraging Unity and MATLAB for control algorithms, the project integrated hardware joysticks and virtual reality for an immersive training experience.

Results

The simulator achieved high-fidelity motion and control dynamics, offering a valuable tool for both hobbyists and aviation enthusiasts.
